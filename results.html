Section 01 — Sea Level Rise
Projected Global Sea Level Rise by Scenario

Using the BRICK model, we generated annual regional sea level projections from 2015 to 2100 under each SSP scenario. As expected, higher-emissions pathways produce significantly greater sea level rise, with divergence between scenarios becoming pronounced after mid-century. Note that under any scenario, a certain amount of sea level rise is to be expected, and even "locked-in". Actions that we take now don't undo our prior negligence, so a certain level of rise is inevitable.

Section 02 — Economic Costs
Projected Global Economic Damages by Scenario

Using the pyCIAM model with our BRICK sea level projections and the SLIIDERS coastal dataset, we estimated total global economic damages for each SSP scenario in 2100. Costs span six categories: permanent inundation, storm damage, mortality, protection expenditures, relocation, and wetland loss.
